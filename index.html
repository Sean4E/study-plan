How priority works

Each topic gets a priority score = workload × (11 − grade prediction). Higher numbers mean more study time. Topics with high workload and low predicted grades climb to the top of the queue. The plan generator uses this — together with how close your exam is — to decide what to schedule first.

Weekly availability

Set your free study window for each day, then add anything that breaks it up — classes that run late, work, training, family stuff. The planner only books sessions in the gaps.
